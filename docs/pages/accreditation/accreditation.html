Office of Licensure, Assessment, and Accreditation

Our Mission

The mission of the Office of Licensure, Assessment, and Accreditation is to support the University by leading activities related to University-wide accreditation and institutional effectiveness.

To accomplish its mission, OAA provides consultation, training, and administrative and technical support related to assessment, including General Education; internal and external academic program change procedures; and strategic planning activities for faculty and staff.

We are seeking accreditation with the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges, and further work with the following schools to ensure proper acceditation of their programs:

  • School of Education, the Council for Accreditation of Counseling and Related Educational Programs
  • School of Health and Human Services, the Council on Social Work Education
  • College of Environmental Sciences, the Society of American Foresters

Associate’s Degree Requirements

The associate’s degree is awarded to students who have successfully met the following requirements:

  1. Successful completion of 61 credit hours, including the following General Education Requirements:
    • GRA-100: First Year Writing
    • LOG-100: First Year Scientific Reasoning
    • RHE-100: First Year Rhetoric
    • MUS-100: Music Appreciation or SPA-100: Art Appreciation
    • One Literature course
    • One Mathematics course
    • One Natural Science course
    • One Social or Behavioral Science course
  2. A grade point average on credit hours attempted of no less than 2.0.
  3. At least 16 credits hours in residence at Romanorum Theological University.

Bachelor’s Degree Requirements

The bachelor’s degree is awarded to students who have successfully met the following requirements:

  1. Successful completion of 122 credit hours, including the following General Education Requirements:
    • GRA-100: First Year Writing
    • LOG-100: First Year Scientific Reasoning
    • RHE-100: First Year Rhetoric
    • MUS-100: Music Appreciation
    • SPA-100: Art Appreciation
    • One History course
    • One Literature course
    • One Mathematics course
    • Two Natural Science courses
    • Two Social or Behavioral Science courses
  2. A grade point average on credit hours attempted of no less than 2.0.
  3. At least 32 credits hours in residence at Romanorum Theological University.
